Subscriptions are quiet. They auto-renew, hide in bank statements, and add up fast.

So I built Sub-Tracker — a personal subscription auditor.

It connects to my bank via Plaid, ingests every transaction, flags the recurring ones, and tells me what they actually cost me per year. When I'm ready to cancel one, it gives me a verified link straight to the cancellation page — no more googling "how to cancel [merchant]."

I self-host it because I don't love the idea of a third party holding my Plaid access tokens.

The app itself was a weekend of code. Most of the time went to four bugs:

— 403 on every Plaid request. My same-origin check trusted req.url, but a reverse proxy silently rewrites the host. Trust x-forwarded-host instead.

— "Internal error" on Connect bank. I'd generated my AES-256 key with openssl rand -base64 48 instead of 32. Half the key was junk.

— Plaid showed "First Platypus Bank" instead of Navy Federal — my env loader had locked me into sandbox.

— Categorize dropdown only had "Uncategorized" because my deploy ran migrations but never the seed.

The app was a weekend.
The bugs were a week.

That ratio is most of what shipping software actually feels like.

Stack: Next.js · Prisma · Postgres · Plaid · self-hosted on Coolify.
